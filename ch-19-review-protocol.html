21  Review Protocol (Human-in-the-Loop)

Blueprint extraction combines automated inference with architectural interpretation. Even with strong evidence models and validation rules, automated extraction cannot fully guarantee that reconstructed structures accurately reflect the system’s architecture. For this reason, human review is an explicit stage in the extraction pipeline.

The review protocol defines how extracted blueprints are examined, corrected, and approved before they become part of a blueprint repository. Rather than treating review as an informal step, the protocol formalizes: what must be reviewed, how review is performed, how decisions are recorded, and when a blueprint becomes usable.

Review integrates human judgment into the extraction pipeline without abandoning automation. Automation performs signal detection, evidence aggregation, and structural reconstruction. Human reviewers validate interpretations, resolve ambiguities, and refine architectural representations.

21.1 Why Human Review Matters

Automated extraction is powerful but has inherent limitations. Human review addresses these limitations by bringing contextual understanding, domain expertise, and architectural judgment to the extraction process.

Automated extraction limitations:

automated_limitations:
  contextual_understanding:
    limitation: "Extraction lacks deep understanding of system context"
    impact: "May misinterpret architectural intent"
    human_value: "Reviewers understand system purpose and context"

  domain_expertise:
    limitation: "Extraction patterns may not match domain conventions"
    impact: "May miss domain-specific architectural patterns"
    human_value: "Reviewers recognize domain-specific designs"

  implicit_conventions:
    limitation: "Extraction struggles with undocumented conventions"
    impact: "May miss structures based on implicit agreements"
    human_value: "Reviewers understand team-specific conventions"

  edge_cases:
    limitation: "Extraction rules may not cover unusual cases"
    impact: "Edge cases may be misinterpreted"
    human_value: "Reviewers handle unusual architectural patterns"

  tradeoffs:
    limitation: "Extraction can't capture architectural tradeoffs"
    impact: "Missing rationale for design decisions"
    human_value: "Reviewers document architectural reasoning"

Review adds value:

review_value_proposition:
  correction:
    automated: "May produce incorrect interpretations"
    review_action: "Correct misinterpretations"
    impact: "Improved accuracy"

  validation:
    automated: "Cannot verify architectural plausibility"
    review_action: "Validate architectural decisions"
    impact: "Increased confidence"

  documentation:
    automated: "Doesn't capture rationale"
    review_action: "Document ADRs and reasoning"
    impact: "Better understanding"

  prioritization:
    automated: "Treats all items equally"
    review_action: "Focus on high-impact uncertainties"
    impact: "Efficient resource use"

  improvement:
    automated: "Cannot improve extraction quality"
    review_action: "Identify areas for method improvement"
    impact: "Better future extractions"

Review as integration, not replacement:

review_integration:
  automation_role: "Perform signal detection, evidence aggregation, structural reconstruction"
  review_role: "Validate interpretations, resolve ambiguities, refine representations"
  
  collaborative_process:
    - step_1: "Extraction produces initial blueprint"
    - step_2: "Validation ensures structural correctness"
    - step_3: "Review validates architectural accuracy"
    - step_4: "Combined output is trusted blueprint"
  
  benefits:
    - "Leverages automation for scale"
    - "Leverages humans for accuracy"
    - "Best of both worlds"

21.2 Slot-Oriented Review Checklist

Review is organized according to the Blueprint Kernel slots. This slot-oriented structure allows reviewers to inspect architectural elements systematically rather than scanning the entire blueprint in an unstructured way.

    2. Capabilities
    3. Components
    4. Interfaces
    5. Data
    6. Workflows

21.3 Priority-Based Review

In large-scale extraction pipelines, not every field requires the same level of review. The review protocol therefore prioritizes fields according to confidence and architectural impact. This ensures that human attention is focused where it matters most.

Priority matrix:

priority_matrix:
  evaluation_dimensions:
    confidence:
      levels:
        - high_confidence: 0.8 - 1.0
        - medium_confidence: 0.4 - 0.8
        - low_confidence: 0.0 - 0.4

    impact:
      levels:
        - critical_impact: "System-critical architectural elements"
        - high_impact: "Major architectural components"
        - medium_impact: "Supporting architectural elements"
        - low_impact: "Minor architectural details"

  priority_calculation:
    high_priority:
      conditions:
        - confidence: low AND impact: critical
        - confidence: medium AND impact: critical
        - confidence: low AND impact: high
      examples:
        - "Component boundaries with low confidence"
        - "Interfaces inferred without explicit contracts"
        - "Data ownership relationships inferred from limited evidence"

    medium_priority:
      conditions:
        - confidence: medium AND impact: high
        - confidence: high AND impact: medium
        - confidence: low AND impact: medium
      examples:
        - "Capability descriptions with medium confidence"
        - "Workflow task dependencies"
        - "Component interface mappings"

    low_priority:
      - conditions:
        - confidence: high AND impact: critical
        - confidence: high AND impact: high
        - confidence: medium AND impact: low
      examples:
        - "Observed API routes"
        - "Observed schema definitions"
        - "Well-documented components"

    very_low_priority:
      - conditions:
        - confidence: high AND impact: low
        - confidence: medium AND impact: low
      examples:
        - "Observed configuration settings"
        - "Well-defined deployment parameters"

Priority review examples:

priority_review_examples:
  high_priority_review:
    item:
      field: "Component boundary between payment_service and payment_worker"
      confidence: 0.65
      impact: critical

    analysis:
      confidence_issue: "Boundary inferred from directory structure and queue dependency"
      impact_issue: "Critical - affects component decomposition"

    review_focus:
      - "Verify boundary is accurate"
      - "Check if components should be merged or separated"
      - "Review deployment configuration"

    action: "Require careful review and possible correction"

  medium_priority_review:
    item:
      field: "Capability 'process_payment'"
      confidence: 0.75
      impact: high

    analysis:
      confidence_issue: "Capability inferred from workflow, not directly documented"
      impact_issue: "High - affects system understanding"

    review_focus:
      - "Verify capability exists and is important"
      - "Check if workflow accurately represents capability"
      - "Consider documenting capability explicitly"

    action: "Review and possibly clarify documentation"

  low_priority_review:
    item:
      field: "API route GET /payments/{id}"
      confidence: 0.98
      impact: high

    analysis:
      confidence_issue: "None - strong evidence"
      impact_issue: "None - critical but well-supported"

    review_focus:
      - "Spot check for accuracy"
      - "Quick verification"

    action: "Minimal review - spot check only"

  very_low_priority_review:
    item:
      field: "Configuration setting: cache timeout"
      confidence: 0.90
      impact: low

    analysis:
      confidence_issue: "None - strong evidence"
      impact_issue: "None - operational detail"

    review_focus:
      - "Quick verification if time permits"
      - "Can be skipped if resource-constrained"

    action: "Optional review - may be skipped"

Review prioritization strategy:

prioritization_strategy:
  resource_allocation:
    principle: "Focus attention where it matters most"
    calculation:
      - "80% of review time on high-priority items"
      - "15% of review time on medium-priority items"
      - "5% of review time on low-priority items"

  review_scheduling:
    step_1: "Identify all review items"
    step_2: "Calculate priority for each item"
    step_3: "Sort items by priority (high → low)"
    step_4: "Review items in priority order"
    step_5: "Mark items as reviewed"

  efficiency_gains:
    - "Critical issues caught early"
    - "Limited human attention on well-supported items"
    - "Maximum impact per review hour"
    - "Scalable to large blueprint datasets"

21.4 Decision Logging

Every significant modification made during review should be recorded. The review protocol therefore requires maintaining a decision log, typically expressed in the form of architecture decision records (ADR).

ADR structure:

adr_structure:
  title: "Descriptive title of the decision"
  status: accepted | rejected | superseded | deprecated
  date: "Date when decision was made"
  decision_makers: ["List of reviewers"]

  context:
    problem: "What problem was being addressed?"
    background: "What context led to this decision?"
    constraints: "What constraints influenced the decision?"

  decision:
    description: "What was decided?"
    rationale: "Why was this decision made?"
    alternatives_considered: "What alternatives were considered?"
      - alternative: "Description"
        pros: ["Pros of this alternative"]
        cons: ["Cons of this alternative"]

  consequences:
    positive: ["Positive outcomes of this decision"]
    negative: ["Negative outcomes or trade-offs"]
    risks: ["Risks introduced by this decision"]

  related_decisions:
    - "Links to related ADRs"

  evidence:
    - "References to supporting evidence"

Decision log example:

decision_log:
  - id: "adr-001"
    title: "Architecture pattern: Monolithic with Worker Separation"
    status: accepted
    date: "2026-03-08"
    decision_makers: ["architect@example.com", "lead_developer@example.com"]

    context:
      problem: "Repository documentation describes microservices architecture, but code shows monolithic application with background workers"
      background: "Documentation was created during initial planning but implementation evolved to monolithic approach"
      constraints: "Extraction must reflect actual implementation, not aspirational documentation"

    decision:
      description: "Treat system as monolithic with service/worker separation rather than true microservices"
      rationale: "Evidence from implementation (strong) outweighs documentation (weak). Architecture should reflect actual code structure, not documentation"
      alternatives_considered:
        - alternative: "Treat as true microservices"
          pros: ["Aligns with documentation", "Scalable architecture"]
          cons: ["No evidence in code", "Would require architectural changes"]
        - alternative: "Treat as modular monolith"
          pros: ["Accurate to code structure", "Simpler architecture"]
          cons: ["May not reflect intended design"]

    consequences:
      positive:
        - "Architecture view accurately reflects implementation"
        - "Deployment shows single application with workers"
        - "No cross-service network boundaries"
      negative:
        - "Doesn't match documentation"
        - "May limit future scalability"
      risks:
        - "Documentation may mislead understanding"
        - "Future refactoring may be needed if scaling required"

    related_decisions: []

    evidence:
      - "evidence-010: FastAPI application initialization (strong)"
      - "e-011: Docker service definition (strong)"
      - "e-012: HTTP route definitions (strong)"
      - "e-013: README documentation (weak)"

  - id: "adr-002"
    title: "Goal statement validation: inferred from capabilities"
    status: accepted
    date: "2026-03-08"
    decision_makers: ["product_manager@example.com"]

    context:
      problem: "Goal is inferred from capabilities rather than explicitly documented"
      background: "Repository lacks explicit goal statement"
      constraints: "Must provide meaningful goal for blueprint"

    decision:
      description: "Accept inferred goal: 'Process payment transactions and manage refunds for e-commerce platforms'"
      rationale: "Goal aligns with extracted capabilities and is reasonable given functionality. Confidence remains lower (0.70) due to lack of explicit documentation"
      alternatives_considered:
        - alternative: "Mark goal as unknown"
          pros: ["Honest about uncertainty"]
          cons: ["Blueprint lacks high-level purpose"]
        - alternative: "Infer broader goal"
          pros: ["More comprehensive"]
          cons: ["May over-interpretation"]

    consequences:
      positive:
        - "Blueprint has meaningful goal statement"
        - "Goal aligns with capabilities"
      negative:
        - "Lower confidence than other slots"
        - "Product view may be incomplete"
      risks:
        - "Goal may not reflect true system purpose"
        - "May require future revision"

    related_decisions: []

    evidence:
      - "e-001: README mention of payment processing (moderate)"
      - "e-003: API endpoint create_payment (strong)"
      - "e-007: API endpoint refund_payment (strong)"

21.5 Blueprint Maturity Gates

After review, blueprints progress through a series of maturity states. These states define the level of reliability associated with the extracted architecture. Maturity gates provide governance for blueprint repositories, ensuring that high-quality architectural representations can be distinguished from preliminary extraction results.

Maturity state definitions:

maturity_states:
  draft:
    description: "Initial output of extraction pipeline"
    characteristics:
      - "Has passed validation checks"
      - "Has not undergone full review"
      - "May contain uncertainties"
      - "May require corrections"
    usage: "Internal analysis only"
    confidence_threshold: "N/A"
    required_actions:
      - "Complete validation"
      - "Begin review process"

  usable:
    description: "Has passed initial review and is sufficiently accurate"
    characteristics:
      - "Passed initial review"
      - "Major issues resolved"
      - "Minor uncertainties may remain"
      - "Suitable for analysis and comparison"
    usage: "Analysis, comparison, understanding"
    confidence_threshold: "> 0.70"
    required_actions:
      - "Complete initial review"
      - "Resolve high-severity issues"
      - "Document remaining uncertainties"

  reference:
    description: "Fully reviewed and verified architecture description"
    characteristics:
      - "Passed comprehensive review"
      - "All issues resolved"
      - "All uncertainties resolved or documented"
      - "Can serve as canonical example"
    usage: "Pattern libraries, canonical examples, dataset inclusion"
    confidence_threshold: "> 0.85"
    required_actions:
      - "Complete comprehensive review"
      - "Resolve all issues"
      - "Document all ADRs"

Maturity gate transitions:

maturity_transitions:
  draft_to_usable:
    trigger: "Initial review completed"
    requirements:
      - "All high-priority items reviewed"
      - "Critical issues resolved"
      - "Major uncertainties addressed"
      - "ADR logged for significant decisions"
    validation:
      - "Confidence threshold: > 0.70"
      - "No critical unresolved issues"
    outcome: "Blueprint promoted to usable"

  usable_to_reference:
    trigger: "Comprehensive review completed"
    requirements:
      - "All items reviewed"
      - "All issues resolved"
      - "All uncertainties resolved or documented"
      - "ADR log complete"
    validation:
      - "Confidence threshold: > 0.85"
      - "No unresolved issues"
      - "Complete ADR coverage"
    outcome: "Blueprint promoted to reference"

  demotion:
    trigger: "Issues discovered after promotion"
    requirements:
      - "New critical issues found"
      - "Evidence contradicts decisions"
      - "Architecture has evolved"
    action: "Demote to lower maturity level"
    outcome: "Blueprint demoted for re-review"

21.6 Human Review as Part of the Pipeline

The review protocol integrates human judgment into the extraction pipeline without abandoning automation. This hybrid approach preserves both scalability and architectural accuracy.

Pipeline integration:

pipeline_integration:
  automated_stage:
    responsibilities:
      - "Signal detection from repository artifacts"
      - "Evidence collection and aggregation"
      - "Ontology mapping and entity extraction"
      - "Kernel construction and filling"
      - "View generation through projection"
      - "Validation: schema, reference, completeness"
    strengths:
      - "Scalable to large repositories"
      - "Consistent processing"
      - "Fast and efficient"
      - "Reproducible results"
    limitations:
      - "Cannot handle ambiguity well"
      - "May miss domain knowledge"
      - "Cannot validate architectural plausibility"

  human_review_stage:
    responsibilities:
      - "Validate architectural interpretations"
      - "Resolve ambiguities and conflicts"
      - "Correct extraction errors"
      - "Document architectural decisions"
      - "Assess and adjust confidence"
    strengths:
      - "Contextual understanding"
      - "Domain expertise"
      - "Architectural judgment"
      - "Ability to handle ambiguity"
    limitations:
      - "Not scalable for massive volumes"
      - "Time-intensive"
      - "Subjective and variable"

  collaboration:
    - "Automation provides initial draft"
    - "Validation ensures structural soundness"
    - "Review validates architectural accuracy"
    - "Result: trusted blueprint"

Review efficiency mechanisms:

21.7 Chapter Summary

  • Human review is an explicit stage in extraction pipeline, not an optional add-on
  • Review addresses automated extraction limitations: context, domain expertise, implicit conventions, edge cases, tradeoffs
  • Review adds value through correction, validation, documentation, prioritization, and improvement
  • Slot-oriented review checklist ensures systematic review of all kernel slots (Goal, Capabilities, Components, Interfaces, Data, Workflows)
  • Each slot has specific review questions and criteria for validation
  • Priority-based review focuses attention on low-confidence, high-impact items for maximum efficiency
  • Priority matrix considers confidence levels and architectural impact
  • Decision logging uses ADR format to document architectural decisions with context, rationale, and consequences
  • ADRs provide transparency, traceability, learning, and auditability
  • Blueprint maturity gates (draft → usable → reference) define reliability levels
  • Draft: passed validation, not reviewed, for internal analysis
  • Usable: passed initial review, suitable for analysis, confidence > 0.70
  • Reference: fully reviewed, verified architecture, confidence > 0.85, for pattern libraries
  • Review integrates human judgment with automation, preserving scalability and accuracy
  • Hybrid approach produces trusted, high-quality architectural knowledge
